Piano Types We Move

🎹 Upright Pianos

Weight: 300-500 lbs

Console, studio, and full-size uprights. Most common piano type in homes. Requires piano board and proper strapping technique.

🎹 Baby Grand Pianos

Weight: 500-600 lbs

4'11" to 5'6" in length. Legs must be removed for safe transport. Requires piano board, skid board, and padding.

🎹 Grand Pianos

Weight: 600-1,200+ lbs

Medium grands (5'7"-6'10") and concert grands (7'+). Lid, legs, and pedal lyre removed. Premium care required.

🎹 Digital Pianos

Weight: 50-300 lbs

Stage pianos and digital grands with stands. Lighter but still require careful handling to protect electronics.

🎹 Antique & Vintage Pianos

Weight: Varies

Player pianos, Victorian uprights, and collectible instruments. Extra care for delicate finishes and mechanisms.

🎹 Organ Moving

Weight: 200-800+ lbs

Church organs, home organs, and Hammond organs. Special handling for tone wheels and electronic components.

Our Piano Moving Process

1

Assessment

We evaluate piano type, weight, location, stairs, doorways, and any access challenges. Photos help us quote accurately.

2

Preparation

Lock keyboard lid, wrap with moving blankets, secure with straps. For grands: remove legs, lid, and pedal lyre.

3

Transport

Piano board for uprights, skid board for grands. Climate-controlled truck protects from Arizona heat. Secured for no movement.

4

Placement

Positioned exactly where you want. For grands: legs and pedal lyre reinstalled, lid replaced, bench delivered.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to move a piano in Phoenix?

Piano moving costs typically start at $250-$350 for local upright piano moves and $400-$800+ for grand pianos. Final pricing depends on piano type, stairs involved, distance, and access difficulty. Contact us for a free quote specific to your piano.

Can you move a grand piano up stairs?

Yes, we move grand pianos up and down stairs using specialized equipment including piano boards, straps, and dollies. Our experienced team handles complex stair moves safely. Additional fees apply for stairs—typically $75-$150 per flight depending on piano size.

Do I need to tune my piano after moving?

We recommend waiting 2-3 weeks after moving before tuning to allow the piano to acclimate to its new environment. Temperature and humidity changes affect tuning—especially important in Arizona's dry climate. We can provide tuner recommendations upon request.

Are you insured for piano moving?

Yes, we carry full liability insurance for all piano moves. Your instrument is protected from pickup to delivery. We treat every piano—from student uprights to concert grands—with the care it deserves.

How long does a piano move take?

Most local piano moves take 1-3 hours depending on complexity. Upright pianos with good access: 1-1.5 hours. Grand pianos requiring disassembly: 2-3 hours. Stairs and tight spaces add time but ensure safety.

What equipment do you use?

Professional piano moving equipment: piano boards, skid boards, heavy-duty straps, moving blankets, piano dollies, and ramps. For grands, we have proper tools for leg removal and reinstallation. Climate-controlled truck option available.

Can you move a piano into storage?

Yes, we can move pianos to storage facilities. We recommend climate-controlled storage to protect from Arizona's extreme temperatures. Humidity control helps prevent soundboard damage and tuning stability issues.

How do I prepare my piano for moving?

Remove items from piano top, lock keyboard lid if possible, clear pathway to truck, and let us know about any existing damage. Don't try to push or tip the piano yourself—that's what we're here for!
