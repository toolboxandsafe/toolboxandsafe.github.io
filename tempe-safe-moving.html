Where Tempe Homeowners Live

South Tempe is the sweet spot. The area south of Baseline Road has Tempe's highest concentration of single-family homeowners. Warner Ranch, Kyrene Corridor, and neighborhoods around Corona del Sol High School are where I do most Tempe safe moves.
  • South Tempe: Established single-family homes from the 80s and 90s. Good layouts, usually straightforward access.
  • Warner Ranch: Master-planned community with larger lots. Families who've been here 15-20+ years.
  • Kyrene Corridor: Top school district attracts families. Many homeowners with permanent safe installations.
  • The Lakes: Townhome and single-family mix near Tempe Town Lake. Tighter access in townhomes — I assess first.

ASU Staff & Faculty

Arizona State University isn't just students. ASU employs thousands of faculty, researchers, and staff — many of whom own homes in Tempe and neighboring cities.

  • Professors and researchers often work from home offices with document safes for sensitive research materials.
  • Long-term residents: ASU employees who've been in Tempe for decades have established collections and permanent safe setups.
  • Retirement transitions: Faculty retiring from ASU sometimes downsize, requiring safe moves to smaller homes or out-of-state.

Tempe-Specific Considerations

ASU Event Traffic

Football Saturdays and major ASU events create serious traffic around Mill Avenue and the stadium. For moves in central Tempe, avoid game days entirely or schedule early morning before event traffic starts.

Older Home Layouts

Tempe's established neighborhoods have homes from the 70s, 80s, and 90s — before modern 36" doorway standards. Some homes have 32" doorways or split-level layouts. Send me photos of tight spots before move day.

Townhomes & Condos

Tempe has more townhomes and condos than surrounding cities. If you're in a multi-story townhome, let me know which floor the safe is on (or going to). Stairwell moves require different equipment.

Summer Moves

Tempe's population drops significantly when ASU is out of session (May-August). This actually makes summer moves easier — less traffic, easier parking, more scheduling flexibility.
