For Mechanics Changing Shops

🔧 I understand your situation.
When you're moving shops, you're losing money every day your box isn't set up. I work around your schedule — early mornings, evenings, weekends. Let's get you back to work.

Common Scenarios

  • Switching dealerships: Friday night pickup, Monday morning delivery. Zero lost work days.
  • Opening your own shop: I can move the box while you're handling everything else.
  • Retiring/downsizing: Moving a box to storage or a buyer's location.
  • Bought a used box: Pickup from seller, deliver to your shop.

Leave It Loaded

You don't have to empty it. Lock the drawers, secure any loose items on top, and I'll move it as-is. Your tools stay organized exactly how you had them.

Obviously a lighter box is easier to move, but I understand that emptying a full Snap-on takes hours. If the drawers lock and nothing's falling out, we're good.

What to Secure

  • Lock all drawers (most toolboxes have a central lock)
  • Remove or secure anything sitting on top
  • If you have a hutch/top box, let me know — those usually move separately

Shop Access

I've moved toolboxes out of tight bays, through back doors, around lifts, and down ramps. Before the move, let me know:

  • Bay location: Which bay is your box in? Any lifts or vehicles blocking access?
  • Door clearance: Will we go through a main bay door or a side entrance?
  • Shop hours: When can I access pickup and delivery locations?
  • Floor surface: Concrete? Epoxy? Any transitions or thresholds?
